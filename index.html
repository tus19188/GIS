Let's Begin

Semester Overview

After completing this course, I gained a comprehensive understanding of the core concepts and practical applications of GIS. I learned different ways to shape, analyze, and visualize spatial information to help solve a wide range of problems. I learned how to use different tools that help sort and filter information to create a new set of spatial information that would be relevant for the problem at hand. What impressed me the most about GIS is the wide range of applications it can have in various fields. On this webpage, I display three of my best maps that serve three different different purposes. Taking this course has opened my eyes about the effectiveness of GIS to help visualize information to address a problem.

Finalizing Grocery Store Placement for the City of Philadelphia

The City of Philadelphia wants to select an area where a new store committed to selling healthy food could be built. This new location must be accessible by SEPTA and in an area where there are no farmers markets/corner stores already in place. Specifically, the new store must be in a Philadelphia empowerment zone, within 2000ft of a SEPTA station, not within 1200ft of a farmerâ€™s market or corner store part of the Healthy Corner Store Initiative, and preferably a large contiguous area to better serve an area and be more likely to secure a property. By ensuring the correct projection is used, creating an inclusive layer that fulfills the criteria, and creating an exclusive layer to then help finalize the areas that need a grocery store, GIS came in handy to determine the candidates for a new grocery store that fulfills the criteria set by the city.

Helping the City of Los Angeles Raise Awareness Inhaling Methane From Landfills, CA

The City of Los Angeles is interested in raising awareness about the possible threats children and young adults may be exposed to by attending schools close to methane-producing landfills since inhaling methane may negatively harm human health. To engage critical stakeholders, the City requested to solve three problems. One of those problems is to determine the supervisorial districts in the bottom 50% of income per capita in a subset of Census Tracts. This subset of Census Tracts must contain schools within 0.5 kilometers of a methane-producing landfill. By using a combination of Select by Attributes and Select by Location, GIS helped to create a subset that narrows the supervisorial districts meeting the criteria requested by the city.

Determining the Areas Most At-Risk for Water Pollution for the City of Flint

The City of Flint wants to address the water crisis by determining who is most at-risk for having access to poisonous water based on a combination of three factors: areas that are within 0.3 miles of a TRI facility that generations over 10,000 tons of water pollution; areas that are within 0.3 miles of a river; and areas that are withing 0.2 miles of water service lines that are made of lead. The City wants the data from the University of Michigan to be used to analyze the toxic water in Flint. GIS helped with determining the areas most at risk by creating a map that uses the correct projection, the joins and the select by features to combine and filter different data sets, the create a layer, create buffers, and intersect tools to highlight the regions where all three criteria are met. Additional tools like the calculate geometry tool were also used to provide the city details about the most-at risk region. Once again, the tools GIS has helped with addressing a critical problem.
