Philadelphia

The School District of Philadelphia (SDP) wants students to bike to school as an alternative way to get to school. To implement this interest, the school district wants to target schools with a large enrollment and are close to the bike network in Philadelphia.To solve the problem, I narrowed the list of schools via Select by Attribute to SDP schools that have an enrollment greater than 1,200 students. Using this selection of data, I narrowed the list of schools further via Select by Location to schools that are 0.1 mile from the Philadelphia Streets Bike Network. Now that we have the number of schools that SDP must target, I highlighted the neighborhoods where these targeted schools are located using Select by location. As a result of stacking these layers, SDP must target five schools and work with four neighborhoods to execute their plan to encourage students to bike to school. Deliverables 1. The list of SDP schools that enroll more than 1,200 students and are within 0.1 mile of the bike network are as follows: a. Baldi, C. C. A. b. Central c. Frank, Anne d. Frankford HS e. Los Angeles, CA

The City of Los Angeles is interested in raising awareness about the possible threats children and young adults may be exposed to by attending schools close to methane-producing landfills. Inhaling methane may negatively harm human health, which is why the City wants to raise awareness. To engage critical stakeholders, three components will be determined. The first component is to determine the supervisorial districts in the bottom 50% of income per capita in a subset of Census Tracts. This subset of Census Tracts will contain schools within 0.5 kilometers of a methane-producing landfill. The second component is to highlight the wealthiest Census Tract that has a methane-producing landfill. The third component is to narrow down the closest landfill to Topeka Charter School for Advanced Studies. These three pieces of information will help the City of Los Angeles raise awareness regarding the potential harm methane-producing landfills close to schools may have on children and young adults. 1. Findings: Two supervisorial districts meet the criteria of component #1. Those supervisorial districts are District 1 and District 2.

Flint, MI

The City of Flint wants to address the water crisis. They want a map that determines who is most at-risk for having access to poisonous water based on a combination of three factors: areas that are within 0.3 miles of a TRI facility that generations over 10,000 tons of water pollution; areas that are within 0.3 miles of a river; and areas that are withing 0.2 miles of water service lines that are made of lead. The City wants the data from the University of Michigan to be used to analyze the toxic water in Flint. To develop a map that narrows who is most at risk for drinking poisonous water, the data was first prepared to be analyzed. The first step was to ensure all the shapefiles had the same projection, NAD_1983_HARN_StatePlane_Michigan_South_FIPS_2113_Feet_Intl . The Michigan TRI Water Pollution table (TRI_Water_Pollution) was then joined with the Michigan TRI facility information (Michigan_TRI_Facilities). This new exported data was then filtered to only display TRI facilities that generate over 10,000 tons of water pollution. Then the service line data (Servicelinedata) was filtered to only demonstrate water service lines made from lead. The data for Michigan rivers was also modified to only include the rivers that run through Flint by using the clip tool. The second part was creating a layer where the three combinations of criteria overlap since the City wants an understanding of where these three components combine. Buffers were created for all three components (TRI facilities with over 10,000 tons of water pollution with a buffer of 0.3 miles, rivers with a buffer of 0.3 miles, and water service lines made of lead with a buffer of 0.3 miles). Then the intersect tool was used to locate the areas where all three buffers overlap, which was named Vulnerable_Area. The last part was to determine the wards most at-risk to have poisonous water. The intersect tool was used to divide split the Vulnerable_Area layer by the different wards in the Flint. Then a new field was created to determine the area of the vulnerable region within each ward with the Calculate Geometry tool. With these parts, the areas most at-risk for access to poisonous water were determined.
